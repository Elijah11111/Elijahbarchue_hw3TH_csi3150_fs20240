Sagrada Família Tour

Explore The Sagrada Família in Barcelona, Spain. It's a large, unfinished Roman Catholic basilica designed by the famous architect Antoni Gaudí. Construction began in 1882, and it continues to this day, with ongoing efforts to complete the intricate and ambitious design. The basilica is a masterpiece of modernist architecture, combining Gothic elements with innovative structural techniques and organic forms inspired by nature. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts millions of visitors annually.Barcelona’s craft beer scene.

Barceloneta beach Tour

Discover Barceloneta Beach. It is one of the most popular and well-known beaches in Barcelona, located in the Barceloneta neighborhood. This beach stretches along the Mediterranean coast and is just a short walk from the city's historic center and attractions. It's a lively area, offering a perfect combination of relaxation and activities, such as swimming, sunbathing, beach sports, and enjoying the nearby seafood restaurants and bars..

ParkGüell Tour

Visit Park Güell, It's a famous public park located in Barcelona, Spain, specifically in the Gràcia district, on Carmel Hill. It is one of the most visited tourist attractions in the city, known for its vibrant colors, organic architecture, and scenic views. Designed by the iconic Catalan architect Antoni Gaudí and built between 1900 and 1914, Park Güell was originally intended to be a residential project, but it evolved into a public park that showcases Gaudí's signature style.
